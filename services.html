    • Got technical?

    Giving value to your data requires knowlegde on technical skills. We start by feature engineering the data. We then use multiple regression, exponential smoothing and ARIMA models, then go further with regression trees, k-nearest neighbors, and neural networks for predictive analytics; discriminant analysis, logistic regression, k-nearest neighbors, classification trees, naive Bayes and neural nets for classification, and association rules for affinity ("market basket") analysis. Use principal components, k-means clustering, and hierarchical clustering to simplify and cluster your data. In prescriptive analytics, let us use decision trees, Monte Carlo simulation, and powerful conventional and stochastic optimization.

    Truth be told that framing your business problem's always going to be more important than knowing all available skills in the literature.
